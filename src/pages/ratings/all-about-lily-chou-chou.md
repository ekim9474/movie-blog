All About Lily Chou-Chou is a devastating but beautiful depiction of teenage loneliness and tragedy. The movie mainly follows Yuichi's difficult journey through junior high, as he is thrown into a deep isolation and ostracized by his classmates. The film pulls no punches in its endemic cruelty; we see a cycle of death and violence that pervades the narrative. The transformation of Hoshino's character from a mild-mannered boy to a monstrous manipulator is the aftermath of his own drowning incident and the witnessing of a car accident, which to him strip away the brutality of death and destruction. From that point forward, Hoshino's inhumanity escalates the undeniable presence of violence; his sexual violence and extortion against Kuno and Tsuda is part of his desire of control like his control over Yuichi. The film is not one-note, as interjections of beauty through music and through nature serve as distractions from their abject loneliness. Musicality is foundational to All About Lily Chou-Chou's evocation of emotion. Throughout the film, classical songs like Arabesque underpin the soft tones of love, regret, and pain which cut through the currents of violence.
